    Customer specific pricing

    The WooCommerce Connector provides the capability for you to synchronize item prices between Business Central and your webstore using the ELEX WooCommerce Role Based Pricing plugin.

    Note

    Note:
    The customer specific pricing feature is only available as a separately purchased per tenant extension. For more information, go to Additional paid features.

    The synchronization supports price and discount groups, as well as specific customer synchronization, enabling businesses to maintain consistent pricing strategies across platforms.

    Key benefits

    The following list describes the key benefits of this feature:

    • Price Automation: Automatically synchronize item prices and discounts, reducing manual input errors.
    • Role-Based Pricing: Leverage WooCommerce's role-based pricing to assign tailored prices or discounts to customer groups directly from Business Central.
    • Real-Time Updates: Ensure your WooCommerce store reflects the latest price adjustments in Business Central without delay.
    • Customizable Groups: Define and manage price and discount groups in Business Central, and map them to WooCommerce roles.

    To set up customer specific pricing

    1. Set up price groups in Business Central. For more information about how to configure your price and discount groups in Business Central, Set up customer price groups and Set up customer discount groups.

    2. Link Business Central to your webstore using the ELEX WooCommerce Role Based Pricing plugin. For more information, go to ELEX WooCommerce Role Based Pricing plugin documentation.

    3. Map Business Central price groups to WooCommerce customer roles.

      To map a Business Central price group to a WooCommerce customer role, perform the following steps:

      1. Choose ![Lightbulb that opens the Tell Me feature.](media/ui-search/search_small.png "Tell me what you want to do"), enter customer specific pricing, and then choose the related link.

        The Customer Specific Pricing page opens.

      2. On the action bar, choose New.

        A new row appears.

      3. In Type, specify the type of pricing group, for example, Customer Price Group or Customer Discount Group.
      4. In Code, specify a unique identifier for the pricing or discount group.
      5. In Webstore User Role, specify the corresponding user role in WooCommerce, for example, administrator.
      6. If applicable, in Webstore Key, specify the metadata key that is used in WooCommerce for identifying role-based pricing.

    4. Perform an item synchronization. For more information, go to Synchronize items.
